Annie Murphy: Exploring Her Personal and Professional Journey

Canadian actress Anne Murphy, born on December 19, 1986, gained widespread recognition for her standout portrayal of Alexis Rose in the sitcom “Schitt’s Creek” (2015–2020).

Her performance earned her critical acclaim, along with a Primetime Emmy Award and a Golden Globe Award nomination.

Murphy has since ventured into diverse roles, including the dark comedy series “Kevin Can F**k Himself” (2021–2022), the second season of the comedy-drama series “Russian Doll” (2022), and the “Joan Is Awful” episode of the anthology series “Black Mirror” (2023).

Additionally, she lent her voice to the character Chelsea the mermaid in the DreamWorks Animation film “Ruby Gillman, Teenage Kraken” (2023).

Early Life

Murphy came into the world in Ottawa, Ontario, on December 19, 1986, to parents who both pursued careers in education.

Raised in this academic environment, she embarked on her educational journey at Elmwood School in Ottawa, where her early passion for performance manifested in various stage productions.

Initially starting her higher education at Queen’s University, Murphy later opted to transfer, ultimately earning a degree in theatre performance from Concordia University.

Her dedication to honing her craft led her to the Canadian Film Centre Actors’ Conservatory, where she received further training.

At the age of 22, Murphy made a pivotal decision to relocate to Los Angeles, driven by her aspiration to establish a flourishing career in acting.
