Career

Big Show signed to World Champion Wrestling in 1995 and used The Giant as a ring name. He defeated Hulk Hogan in his professional debut fight and claimed the WCW World Heavyweight Champion title. After a few days, the title was taken back from Big Show due to the very controversial end of the match. He attempted again to reclaim the title at WW3 but failed, and Randy Savage won the title. Big show reclaimed the WCW World Heavyweight Champion title by defeating Flair.

Big Show then joined New World Order, but he was thrown out of the New World Order due to his conflict with Hogan. After that, he fought against the New World Order and won WCW World Tag Team Championship twice with different partners. In 1998, Big Show and Nash met in the ring, and Nash defeated the Big Show. Nash quit the New World Order and formed his own New World Order Wolfpac. Big Show rejoined the New World Order and opposed Nash and his partners.

In 1999, Big Show signed a ten-year contract with WWF/E. In June 1999, Big Show fought against Undertaker for the WWF Championship title. Undertaker heavily defeated the Big Show and retained the title. They make an alliance together and won the WWF Tag Team Championship twice. Shortly after that, he featured in triple threat match and won the WWF Championship title.

In 2004, he and Eddie met in the ring, and Big Show defeated him for United States Championship. In 2005, he teamed with Kane, and together they won many fights. He left the WWE due to injuries and later rejoined in 2008. In 2014, at Survivor Series, he knocked out Cena, which lead to his elimination. The big show also featured in several movies and video games.
