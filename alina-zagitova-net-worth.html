Alina Zagitova | Career

She made her international debut at France’s 2016–17 ISU Junior Grand Prix.

Her total score of 194.37 points at the event was the second-best ever earned by a junior female single skater.

Zagitova finished first after the short program at the 2017 World Junior Championships in Taipei with 70.58 points.

She also took first position and a gold medal in the free program.

Moreover, she established two new world records, 138.02 for junior women’s free skating and 208.60 for the combined total.

Alina started the 2017–18 season with a CS Lombardia Trophy victory, finishing third in brief but first in the free with an overall point of 218.46.

At the start of the 2018–19 season, the ISU statistics were reset, with all record holders before 1 July 2018 being historical records.

She won gold with a maximum of 238.43 points after finishing first in the team event and free skate contests.
