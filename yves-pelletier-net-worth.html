Yves Pelletier, a luminary in Canadian entertainment, embarked on his career with the comedy troupe Rock et Belles Oreilles (RBO), where his knack for absurd humor quickly made waves.

Pelletier’s ability to breathe life into characters like Monsieur Caron and Stromgol not only showcased his acting prowess but also set the stage for a diverse career path that would span decades.

From Sketch Comedy to the Silver Screen

FAQs about Yves Pelletier

Who is Yves Pelletier?

He is a Canadian film director, actor, and comedian, recognized for his work with the comedy troupe Rock et Belles Oreilles (RBO).

When was Yves Pelletier born?

He was born on January 15, 1961, in Laval, Quebec, Canada.

What are some of his notable works?

He has appeared in films such as Ding et Dong, le film (1990), Karmina (1996), and Karmina 2 (2001). He also directed movies like Love and Magnets (Les Aimants) in 2004 and Face Time (Le Baiser du Barbu) in 2010.

Has he won any awards?

Yes, he has been honored with four Gémeaux Awards for his work on La Petite Vie and Camping Sauvage.
