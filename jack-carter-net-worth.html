Jack Carter Full Overview and Wiki

The Journey Begins

Born in Brighton Beach, Brooklyn, in 1922, Carter’s foray into entertainment was almost predestined with performances starting as early as three years old atop his parents’ candy store tables.

His early exposure to showbiz set the stage for a career that would span across several facets of entertainment, from comedy to acting to television presenting.

Military Service and Early Career

His commitment to his country, serving in the United States Army Air Forces during World War II, didn’t deter his ambitions in entertainment.

Post-service, he swiftly transitioned into the burgeoning television scene, hosting the early variety program Cavalcade of Stars on the DuMont Network, before moving to NBC with The Jack Carter Show.

Broadway and Beyond

His versatility shone through on Broadway, notably opposite Sammy Davis Jr. in Mr. Wonderful and as a replacement for Phil Silvers in Top Banana.

These roles highlighted Carter’s adaptability and appeal, contributing significantly to his career’s upward trajectory.

A Staple on Television

His television presence was ubiquitous in the 1960s and 1970s, becoming a household name through frequent guest spots on The Ed Sullivan Show and his memorable Ed Sullivan impression.

His knack for comedy and impeccable timing made him a favored panelist on shows like Match Game and a recurring guest on the Dean Martin Celebrity Roasts, further cementing his place in the annals of TV history.

Venturing into Acting and Voice Work

Beyond live performances, Carter ventured into acting, with notable appearances in series like The Love Boat and films such as the cult horror Alligator (1980).

His adaptability allowed him to transition smoothly into voice work later in his career, contributing to animated series like King of the Hill and Family Guy.

Personal Life and Death

His personal life was as colorful and eventful as his career.

He was married three times—first to Joan Mann, then to Paula Stewart, and finally to Roxanne Carter—reflecting a life full of passion both on and off the stage.

He was a father to four children: Chase, Vicki, Michael, and Wendy Chakrin, showcasing a family life that, despite the demands of his career, remained central to his existence.

His life journey came to a peaceful close on June 28, 2015, just four days after his 93rd birthday. He passed away at his home in Beverly Hills, California, due to respiratory failure.

FAQs about Jack Carter

Who was Jack Carter?

He was an American comedian, actor, and television presenter known for his rapid-fire style of comedy. He was a prominent figure in early US television and enjoyed a career spanning over half a century in TV, nightclubs, films, and on stage.

When was Jack Carter born and when did he die?

He was born on June 24, 1922, and passed away on June 28, 2015, just four days after his 93rd birthday.

What were some of his most notable TV appearances?

Carter appeared on many television series such as The Dick Van Dyke Show, Diagnosis: Unknown, Combat!, The Love Boat, Mr. Smith Goes to Washington, The Rockford Files, Emergency!, Cannon, Desperate Housewives, and Shameless, among others.

Did he have any roles on Broadway?

Yes, he acted on Broadway in productions like Call Me Mister (1946), Top Banana (1951), and Mr. Wonderful (1956), where he starred opposite Sammy Davis Jr. He also played Fagin in a stage performance of Oliver! in 1981.

Was he involved in any movies?

He made nearly three dozen films, including roles in Viva Las Vegas, where he played himself, and History of the World, Part I as the Rat Vendor.

What kind of comedy did he perform?

His comedy was known for its brash, caustic style. He often attacked the audience, disparaged politicians and celebrities, and was recognized for his adept ad-libbing skills.
